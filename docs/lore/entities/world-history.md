# World History

AFK Raiders history is deliberately unreliable. Survivors remember the old world through broken signs, inherited grudges, brand jingles, and one guy in Desperanza who insists he has the real story because he found a binder.

The point is not a solemn timeline. The point is a playable excuse for why the world is full of dangerous machines, valuable garbage, civic paperwork, and raiders who think one more crate will fix their lives.

## Folk Eras

| Era | Canon role | Content hooks |
|---|---|---|
| The Before-ish | The comfortable old world, reconstructed from packaging and ads that survived better than people did. | Defunct brand loot, ruined storefronts, old slogans that aged badly. |
| The Bad Years | The remembered disaster period. Nobody agrees on the order of events, but everyone agrees it was loud, expensive, and poorly managed. | Hub gossip, conflicting lore snippets, recovery paperwork jokes. |
| The Great Downstairs | Survivors retreat into Desperanza and declare that a tunnel with labels is basically civilization. | Hub events, hatches, tube routes, ration desks, civic stamps. |
| The First Sort-Of Plan | Early raiders organize surface expeditions with heroic intent and deeply inadequate checklists. | Deployment flavor, field depot jokes, early Raider tradition. |
| The Current Problem | The present day: autonomous raiders, Handler Signal, unstable zones, and an economy based on extracting objects of suspicious value. | Core comms log, stash flavor, raid outcomes, progression text. |

## Writing Rules

- Treat history as campfire canon: confident, contradictory, and useful for jokes.
- Do not explain the full apocalypse unless a feature needs it.
- Old-world references should appear as brands, warnings, procedures, or broken infrastructure.
- Player-facing history should make the current raid funnier, not pause the game for homework.

## Runtime Uses

- Hub gossip can cite fake eras without explaining them.
- Loot flavor can imply old-world priorities were ridiculous before everything broke.
- Zone conditions can reference old systems still trying to do their jobs.
- Achievements can frame terrible habits as proud survivor traditions.
